Gaming PC Running Slow? DK Upgrade And Repair Guide

Many customers visit DK Computer and Gaming Zone because their PC feels slow, games lag, Windows takes too long to start, or the system heats up during gaming. The best fix depends on the real cause, so this guide explains what to check before spending money.

1. When an SSD upgrade is the best fix

If Windows boots slowly, apps open late, or the laptop feels old even after cleaning, an SSD upgrade is usually the first smart step. It makes startup, game loading and daily browsing much faster.

2. When RAM matters

If games stutter while Discord, browser tabs or recording apps are open, the system may need more RAM. For most gaming setups, 16GB is a better target than 8GB.

3. When a graphics card upgrade helps

If the PC starts quickly but FPS is low in games, the GPU may be the limit. Before upgrading, check the power supply, case space and monitor resolution so the card matches the build.

4. When repair or cleaning is needed

Heating, sudden shutdowns, fan noise and random restarts often need service before upgrades. Dust cleanup, thermal paste and cable checks can protect the system and improve performance.

DK tip: Do not upgrade blindly. First identify whether the issue is storage, RAM, graphics, heat, power, or software.

Customer Questions And Answers

Question: My PC gives low FPS. What should I check first?

Answer: Check GPU usage, RAM, storage type, temperature and game settings. If temperatures are high, service comes before upgrade.

Question: Is an SSD better than adding more RAM?

Answer: For slow boot and loading, SSD is usually better. For multitasking and gaming stutter, RAM can help more.

Question: Which accessories are worth buying first?

Answer: Start with a comfortable headset or mic, a reliable keyboard, and a mouse that fits your hand. RGB is nice, but comfort matters first.

Question: Can DK help choose compatible parts?

Answer: Yes. Bring your current PC specs or visit the store so the upgrade matches your motherboard, power supply and case.
